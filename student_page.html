Rules and Regulations

  • Objective: The objective of the game is to test the players' knowledge of programming concepts and languages.

  • Format: The game can be played individually or in teams. Each player/team will be asked a series of questions related to programming.

  • Questions: Questions can be multiple-choice, true/false, fill in the blanks, or open-ended. They should cover a wide range of programming topics, such as syntax, data structures, algorithms, and programming languages.

  • Difficulty Levels: Questions can be categorized into different difficulty levels, such as easy, medium, and hard. This allows players to choose their preferred level of challenge.

  • Time Limit: Each player/team should be given a specific time limit to answer each question. This keeps the game engaging and adds a sense of urgency.

  • Scoring: Players/teams earn points for each correct answer. The points can be awarded based on the difficulty level of the question. For example, harder questions can carry more points.

  • Bonus Rounds:You can include bonus rounds where players /teams have the opportunity to earn additional points. These rounds can include coding challenges or puzzles that require problem-solving skills.
